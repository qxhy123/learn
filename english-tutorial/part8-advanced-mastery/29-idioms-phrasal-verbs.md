# 第29章：习语与短语动词——英语的血肉与灵魂

> "A language without idioms is like a garden without flowers — technically functional, but lacking the beauty that makes it worth dwelling in."
> —— 改编自语言学家 Steven Pinker 关于语言丰富性的论述

---

## 引言

当你能够流利地背诵语法规则，却在听到 "it's raining cats and dogs" 时茫然四顾，或者在老板说 "let's touch base" 时不知所措——这正是习语（Idioms）与短语动词（Phrasal Verbs）的挑战所在。它们是英语的"地下网络"：字面意义一文不值，但比喻意义无处不在。据统计，一个以英语为母语的成年人平均掌握约25,000个习语，每天的对话中至少使用4-6个。掌握习语与短语动词，是从"正确英语"迈向"地道英语"的关键一跃。

---

## 29.1 习语（Idioms）的定义与特点

### 什么是习语

习语是一种**固定表达（fixed expression）**，其整体含义无法从各组成词的字面意义推断出来。它是语言文化的结晶，承载着历史、传统与集体记忆。

| 特征 | 说明 | 例子 |
|------|------|------|
| 语义不透明性 | 整体意义 ≠ 各词意义之和 | "kick the bucket" 踢水桶 → 死亡 |
| 结构固定性 | 不可随意替换词汇 | "spill the beans" 不能说 "pour the beans" |
| 文化依附性 | 与特定文化历史紧密关联 | "take the bull by the horns" 源自斗牛 |
| 语域特定性 | 适用于特定场合（正式/非正式） | "bite the dust" 多用于非正式场合 |

### 字面意义 vs 比喻意义

```
字面意义（Literal Meaning）：
"She has cold feet."
→ 她的脚是冰凉的。（描述体温）

比喻意义（Figurative Meaning）：
"She has cold feet about the wedding."
→ 她对婚礼感到犹豫/临阵退缩。
```

```
字面意义：
"He let the cat out of the bag."
→ 他把猫从袋子里放了出来。

比喻意义：
"He let the cat out of the bag about the surprise party."
→ 他无意中泄露了惊喜派对的秘密。
```

### 习语的变体与灵活性

部分习语允许有限度的变体：

- "break the ice" → "an ice-breaker"（破冰之举/开场白）
- "bite the bullet" → "biting the bullet"（忍痛坚持）
- "burn bridges" / "burn one's bridges" / "burn bridges behind oneself"

## 29.2 英语习语的来源分类

### 来自圣经（Biblical Origins）

英语受基督教文化深刻影响，大量习语源自《圣经》，尤其是《旧约》和《新约》。

| 习语 | 字面来源 | 比喻含义 | 例句 |
|------|----------|----------|------|
| the writing on the wall | 但以理书：神迹般的文字出现在墙上预示亡国 | 不祥预兆；大势已去 | The writing is on the wall for the failing company. |
| a wolf in sheep's clothing | 马太福音：披着羊皮的狼 | 伪装成好人的坏人 | Be careful of him — he's a wolf in sheep's clothing. |
| the salt of the earth | 马太福音：耶稣称门徒为"地上的盐" | 善良朴实、脚踏实地的人 | Farmers are the salt of the earth. |
| bite the dust | 旧约诗篇 | 失败；倒下；死亡 | Another startup has bitten the dust. |
| turn the other cheek | 马太福音：以德报怨 | 宽恕；不还击 | She turned the other cheek when insulted. |
| a drop in the bucket / ocean | 以赛亚书 | 杯水车薪 | Our donation is just a drop in the bucket. |
| forbidden fruit | 创世记：伊甸园禁果 | 禁忌之物（反而更诱人） | Power can be forbidden fruit to the young. |
| the apple of one's eye | 申命记、撒母耳记 | 心肝宝贝；掌上明珠 | His daughter is the apple of his eye. |
| go the extra mile | 马太福音 | 付出额外努力 | She always goes the extra mile for clients. |
| a scapegoat | 利未记：替罪羊仪式 | 替罪羊；背黑锅的人 | He was made the scapegoat for the crisis. |
| cast pearls before swine | 马太福音 | 明珠暗投；对牛弹琴 | Explaining art to him is casting pearls before swine. |

### 来自莎士比亚（Shakespearean Origins）

莎士比亚（1564-1616）创造了数百个沿用至今的习语，是英语习语最重要的单一来源之一。

| 习语 | 出处 | 比喻含义 | 例句 |
|------|------|----------|------|
| break the ice | 《驯悍记》 | 打破僵局；开场白 | Let me break the ice with a joke. |
| wild goose chase | 《罗密欧与朱丽叶》 | 徒劳无功的追逐 | Searching for him was a wild goose chase. |
| green-eyed monster | 《奥赛罗》 | 嫉妒 | Jealousy is the green-eyed monster. |
| cold-blooded | 《威尼斯商人》 | 冷血的；残忍的 | It was a cold-blooded act. |
| vanish into thin air | 《暴风雨》 | 凭空消失 | The money vanished into thin air. |
| wear one's heart on one's sleeve | 《奥赛罗》 | 感情外露；心直口快 | She wears her heart on her sleeve. |
| all that glitters is not gold | 《威尼斯商人》 | 闪光的未必是金子 | Remember: all that glitters is not gold. |
| foregone conclusion | 《奥赛罗》 | 早已注定的结果 | The election result was a foregone conclusion. |
| play fast and loose | 《爱的徒劳》 | 耍手段；不守规矩 | He played fast and loose with the rules. |
| good riddance | 《特洛伊罗斯与克瑞西达》 | 终于摆脱了（某烦人之物） | Good riddance to bad rubbish! |
| method in one's madness | 《哈姆雷特》 | 疯狂之中有逻辑 | There's method in his madness. |
| be-all and end-all | 《麦克白》 | 最重要的事；一切 | Money isn't the be-all and end-all. |

### 来自航海（Nautical Origins）

英国曾是海上强国，航海词汇深入日常语言。

| 习语 | 航海含义 | 比喻含义 | 例句 |
|------|----------|----------|------|
| all at sea | 在茫茫大海中迷失方向 | 困惑；不知所措 | I'm all at sea with this new software. |
| taken aback | 风从正前方吹来，帆被风鼓向后 | 措手不及；吃惊 | She was taken aback by his rudeness. |
| give a wide berth | 停泊时留出足够距离 | 敬而远之；刻意回避 | I give that aggressive dog a wide berth. |
| on an even keel | 船体平稳不倾斜 | 平稳；稳定 | Keep your life on an even keel. |
| in the doldrums | 赤道无风带，船无法行进 | 意志消沉；停滞不前 | The economy is in the doldrums. |
| plain sailing | 顺风顺水的航行 | 轻而易举；一帆风顺 | After that, it was plain sailing. |
| show one's true colors | 战船升起真实旗帜 | 显露本性；原形毕露 | Under pressure, he showed his true colors. |
| three sheets to the wind | 帆绳松动，船行不稳 | 喝得酩酊大醉 | He came home three sheets to the wind. |

| high and dry | 船搁浅，退潮后悬空 | 陷入困境；被遗弃 | They left me high and dry without support. |

### 来自体育（Sports Origins）

| 习语 | 体育来源 | 比喻含义 | 例句 |

## 29.4 短语动词（Phrasal Verbs）的构成规律

### 三种基本类型

```
1. 动词 + 介词（Verb + Preposition）
   "transitive" — 必须有宾语，不可分离
   例：look after (照顾), run into (偶遇), deal with (处理)

   "He ran into an old friend."（正确）
   NOT: "He ran an old friend into."（错误）

2. 动词 + 副词（Verb + Adverb/Particle）
   可分离型（separable）— 宾语可以置于中间或后面
   例：turn off (关掉), pick up (拾起), put on (穿上)

   "Turn off the light."（正确）
   "Turn the light off."（正确）
   "Turn it off."（正确 — 代词必须置于中间）
   NOT: "Turn off it."（错误）

3. 动词 + 副词 + 介词（Three-part Phrasal Verbs）
   通常不可分离
   例：put up with (忍受), look forward to (期待), run out of (用完)

   "I can't put up with this noise."（正确）
   NOT: "I can't put up this noise with."（错误）
```

### 可分离 vs 不可分离——代词位置规则

这是学习短语动词时最重要的语法规则之一：

| 类型 | 宾语为名词 | 宾语为代词 | 例子 |
|------|------------|------------|------|
| 可分离（V+Particle） | 名词前后均可 | 代词只能居中 | Turn the TV off / Turn off the TV → Turn **it** off |
| 不可分离（V+Prep） | 只能置于后面 | 只能置于后面 | Look after the children → Look after **them** |
| 三词短语 | 只能置于后面 | 只能置于后面 | Put up with the noise → Put up with **it** |

**关键记忆点**：当宾语是代词（it, him, her, them）时，可分离型短语动词**必须**将代词放在动词和小品词之间。

## 29.5 高频短语动词深度解析

### GET 系列（最高频之一）

```
get up — 起床；起立
  I usually get up at 7 a.m.
  Please get up — the bus is here.
  She got up to give her seat to the elderly man.

get over — 克服；从……中恢复
  It took months to get over the breakup.
  She can't get over how rude he was.
  Get over it! （振作起来！别想了！）

get along (with) — 相处融洽；进展
  How are you getting along with your new roommate?
  We get along well despite our differences.
  How's the project getting along? （项目进展如何？）

get through — 度过（困难）；通过；接通
  How did you get through such a tough year?
  I finally got through to customer service after 30 minutes.
  Did you get through the entrance exam?

get away with — 逃脱惩罚；侥幸为之
  He got away with cheating for years.
  You won't get away with this!
  She always gets away with coming in late.

get on (British) / get along — 进展；相处
  How are you getting on in your new job?
  They don't get on with each other.
  Age is getting on (with) him.

get out of — 逃避；从……中出来
  He's trying to get out of doing the dishes.
  I can't get out of this commitment.
  What do you hope to get out of this course?

get down to — 认真对待；开始
  Let's get down to business.
  It's time to get down to some serious studying.

get by — 勉强度日；应付过去
  With my low salary, I can barely get by.
  His French is basic, but he gets by.

get rid of — 摆脱；处理掉
  We need to get rid of all this old furniture.
  How do you get rid of a stubborn cold?
  She finally got rid of him (broke up with him).

get into — 进入；对……感兴趣；陷入
  She got into Harvard on a full scholarship.
  I've really gotten into yoga lately.
  How did you get into this mess?

get ahead — 取得成功；超前
  Hard work and persistence help you get ahead.
  She's getting ahead in her career rapidly.

get back — 回来；取回；反击
  When will you get back from your trip?
  I need to get my deposit back.
  I'll get back at him for that insult.

get away — 逃脱；度假
  The thief managed to get away.
  We need to get away for a weekend.
  Get away! （滚开！/ 不会吧！）

get together — 聚会；团聚
  We should get together for lunch sometime.
  The whole family gets together at Christmas.
```

  There was so much to take in at the lecture.

  Who can I turn to for advice?

## 29.6 习语与短语动词的学习策略

### 语境学习法（Contextual Learning）

**避免孤立记忆**。每个习语/短语动词都应在真实语境中学习：

```
低效方法：
"break the ice" = 打破僵局 ✓ 记住了
但明天就忘了，因为没有情感联结。

高效方法：
观看《老友记》第一集：Ross对Rachel说"I remember you had a huge crush on me..."
→ 回忆起那种破冰时的尴尬紧张
→ 下次遇到"break the ice"，立刻唤起画面记忆
```

**实践步骤**：
1. 每天从真实材料（新闻/电影/播客）中找1-2个新习语
2. 找3个不同语境中该习语的使用例子
3. 写一个与自己生活相关的例句
4. 使用Anki卡片定期复习（正面：习语；背面：含义+例句）

### 视觉联想法（Visual Association）

对于不透明的习语，创造视觉图像：

```
"kick the bucket" = 死亡
→ 想象：一个年迈老人愤愤地踢倒水桶，然后倒下——极度夸张的视觉
→ 每次看到这个短语，这个画面就会出现

"burning bridges" = 自断后路
→ 想象：自己站在一座正在燃烧的桥上，无路可退
→ 画面的紧迫感帮助你记住含义
```

### 优先级学习顺序

```
第一优先级（日常对话必备）：
get up, turn on/off, look for, put on/off, come back,
go out, sit down, pick up, give up, take off...

第二优先级（职场/学术常用）：
draw up (起草), put forward (提议), follow up (跟进),
run out of (用尽), deal with (处理), rely on (依赖)...

第三优先级（习语表达）：
从各来源类别中选取高频习语，按使用频率排序学习
```

## 29.7 中英习语对比

中文和英语都有丰富的习语系统，常常有"异曲同工"的对应表达：

| 中文习语 | 英文对应 | 共同含义 | 英文例句 |
|----------|----------|----------|----------|
| 雪中送炭 | a friend in need is a friend indeed | 患难见真情 | He helped when no one else would — a friend in need indeed. |
| 画蛇添足 | gild the lily | 多此一举；弄巧成拙 | Adding more sugar to the cake would be gilding the lily. |
| 半斤八两 | six of one, half a dozen of the other | 半斤八两；差不多 | Both plans have flaws — it's six of one, half a dozen of the other. |
| 亡羊补牢 | close / lock the stable door after the horse has bolted | 事后补救已晚 | Introducing safety rules after the accident is closing the stable door after the horse has bolted. |
| 如鱼得水 | in one's element | 如鱼得水；得心应手 | She's in her element when teaching. |
| 塞翁失马 | every cloud has a silver lining | 坏事中有好事 | Losing that job led to a better one — every cloud has a silver lining. |
| 一石二鸟 | kill two birds with one stone | 一举两得 | Taking that course kills two birds with one stone — skills and credits. |
| 掩耳盗铃 | burying one's head in the sand | 自欺欺人 | Ignoring climate change is burying one's head in the sand. |
| 半途而废 | give up halfway; leave something unfinished | 功亏一篑 | Don't throw in the towel halfway through. |
| 班门弄斧 | teach your grandmother to suck eggs | 班门弄斧 | Don't explain basic programming to him — that's teaching grandmother to suck eggs. |
| 三人成虎 | there's no smoke without fire | 流言可信（有其因必有其果）| People are suspicious — there's no smoke without fire. |
| 同甘共苦 | through thick and thin | 患难与共 | True friends stand by you through thick and thin. |

**文化差异说明**：

中英习语的核心差异在于**文化背景**：
- 中文习语多来自历史典故（如"画蛇添足"出自《战国策》）
- 英文习语多来自宗教、文学、日常劳动场景
- 直接翻译常常导致误解，需要理解背后的文化语境

## 练习

### 练习一：识别习语含义

将下列习语与其含义匹配：

1. "The new employee is learning the ropes."
2. "The weather was the last straw."
3. "His speech really broke the ice."
4. "She's been burning the midnight oil."

**参考答案**：1-熟悉工作流程；2-压垮骆驼的最后一根稻草；3-打破僵局；4-熬夜努力工作

### 练习二：短语动词填空

用适当的 GET / TAKE / PUT / TURN 系列短语动词填空：

1. I need to _____ the lights _____ before sleeping. (turn)
2. She finally _____ _____ her illness after two weeks. (get)
3. He _____ _____ too much work last quarter. (take)
4. I can't _____ _____ _____ his constant criticism. (put)

**参考答案**：1-turn...off；2-got over；3-took on；4-put up with

### 练习三：代词替换

将名词宾语改为代词，注意位置：

1. "Turn off the TV." → Turn _____ _____. 
2. "She picked up the book." → She picked _____ _____.
3. "They put off the meeting." → They put _____ _____.

**参考答案**：1-Turn it off；2-picked it up；3-put it off
