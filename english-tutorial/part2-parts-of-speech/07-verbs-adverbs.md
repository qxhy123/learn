# 第7章：动词与副词——句子的引擎与调速器

> "A verb is the most important word in a sentence. Everything else orbits around it."
> —Steven Pinker（改述）
>
> 如果句子是一台机器，动词就是发动机——没有动词，句子就无法运转。副词则是调速器，它告诉我们动作发生的时间、地点、方式和程度。本章将系统梳理英语动词体系，这是英语学习中最复杂也最值得深入的领域。

---

## 7.1 动词分类总览

英语动词（Verb）的分类体系从功能角度可以划分为四大类：

```
英语动词
├── 实义动词（Lexical Verbs / Main Verbs）
│   ├── 及物动词（Transitive Verbs）：需要宾语
│   └── 不及物动词（Intransitive Verbs）：不需要宾语
├── 系动词（Linking Verbs / Copular Verbs）
├── 助动词（Auxiliary Verbs）
│   ├── 基本助动词：be / do / have
│   └── 情态助动词（Modal Auxiliaries）：can/could/may/might/will/would/shall/should/must/ought to
└── 半助动词（Semi-modals / Marginal Modals）
    例：used to, need to, dare to, be going to, be able to
```

### 7.1.1 系动词（Linking Verbs）

系动词连接主语和表语，本身几乎没有实质意义，主要起连接作用。

```
核心系动词 be（最基础）：
    She is a doctor.        He was tired.

感官系动词（可用 seem/appear 替换检验）：
    look      She looks happy.           （看起来）
    sound     That sounds interesting.   （听起来）
    smell     The flowers smell wonderful.（闻起来）
    taste     The soup tastes delicious. （尝起来）
    feel      The fabric feels soft.     （摸起来 / 感觉）

变化类系动词：
    become    She became a nurse.        （成为）
    get       It's getting cold.         （变得）
    turn      The leaves turned yellow.  （变成）
    go        He went pale.              （变得，通常指不好的变化）
    grow      She grew tired.            （逐渐变得）
    fall      He fell asleep.            （进入某种状态）
    come      His dream came true.       （变为现实）

保持类系动词：
    remain    She remained calm.         （保持）
    stay      Keep/stay healthy!
    keep      Please keep quiet.
```

**系动词的判断技巧：** 将动词替换为 be，如果句意基本不变，则该动词是系动词。

```
She looks tired. → She is tired. ✓（looks = is，系动词）
She looks at the stars. → She is at the stars. ✗（looks ≠ is，实义动词）
```

## 7.2 及物动词与不及物动词

### 7.2.1 及物动词（Transitive Verbs）

及物动词（Vt）需要宾语来完成意义，动作从主语"传递"到宾语。

```
直接宾语（Direct Object）：动作的直接承受者
    She wrote a letter.           （letter 是直接宾语）
    He broke the window.
    They discussed the problem.

间接宾语（Indirect Object）：动作的间接受益/受损者
    She gave him a book.          （him = 间接宾语，a book = 直接宾语）
    He told me a secret.
    She bought her daughter a toy.

双宾语结构 ↔ 介词短语结构：
    She gave him a book.     ↔    She gave a book to him.
    He bought her flowers.   ↔    He bought flowers for her.
    （注意：to 用于 give/send/tell/show/teach/lend/offer 等）
    （for 用于 buy/make/cook/find/get/choose 等）
```

### 7.2.2 不及物动词（Intransitive Verbs）

不及物动词（Vi）不需要宾语，动作不传递给其他对象。

```
不及物动词示例：
    The sun rises.            The baby is sleeping.
    She smiled.               He arrived late.
    They laughed.             The door opened.

常见错误（误加宾语）：
    ❌ The accident happened us.
    ✓  The accident happened to us.
    
    ❌ He arrived the station at noon.
    ✓  He arrived at the station at noon.

    ❌ She listened the music.
    ✓  She listened to the music.
```

### 7.2.3 兼作及物/不及物动词

许多英语动词既可及物也可不及物，意义可能相同或不同：

```
意义相同：
    She is reading (a book).    （她在读（书））
    I eat (breakfast) at 7.     （我7点吃（早饭））

意义不同（重要区分）：
    The door opened.            （门开了，Vi，强调状态变化）
    She opened the door.        （她开了门，Vt，强调动作施加）

    The ice melted.             （冰融化了，Vi）
    The sun melted the ice.     （太阳融化了冰，Vt）

特殊：同根动词的及物/不及物差异
    lie（躺，Vi）→ lay（放置，Vt）→ laid（过去式）
    lie（躺）lie-lay-lain        （不及物）
    lay（放）lay-laid-laid       （及物，需要宾语）

    ❌ Please lay down and rest.
    ✓  Please lie down and rest.
    ✓  Please lay the book on the table.
```

## 7.3 情态动词深度解析

情态动词（Modal Auxiliaries）是英语最具表达层次感的部分——同样一件事，用不同的情态动词，态度和语气完全不同。

### 7.3.1 情态动词的共同特征

```
1. 后接动词原形（不加 to，except ought to）
2. 无第三人称单数 -s（not "he cans" → he can）
3. 无现在分词/过去分词形式
4. 疑问句/否定句不需要助动词 do
5. 有时态对应关系（can→could, will→would, shall→should, may→might）
   但这种对应关系不总是表示过去时！
```

### 7.3.2 情态动词语义层次详解

**CAN / COULD**

```
can：
├── 能力（Ability）：I can swim.（我会游泳）
├── 许可（Permission，非正式）：Can I leave early?
├── 可能性（Possibility，理论上）：Mistakes can happen to anyone.
└── 请求（Request，非正式）：Can you help me?

could：
├── 过去能力：He could run very fast when he was young.
├── 现在/将来的可能性（语气更委婉）：It could rain tomorrow.
├── 礼貌请求（比 can 更正式）：Could you open the window?
├── 过去在特定场合未实现的能力（could + have + pp）：
│   She could have won if she had tried harder.
└── 委婉建议：You could try a different approach.
```

**MAY / MIGHT**

```
may：
├── 许可（正式）：You may leave now.（你可以离开了）
├── 可能性（~50%）：It may rain this afternoon.
├── 让步（正式书面）：Whatever may happen, stay calm.
└── 祝愿：May you have a wonderful journey!

might：
├── 过去的可能性：She thought it might be true.
├── 现在/将来可能性（比 may 更不确定，~30%）：
│   It might rain. （更不确定）
├── 礼貌请求（非常正式）：Might I ask a question?
└── might + have + pp（对过去的推测）：
    He might have left already. （他可能已经离开了）
```

**MUST / HAVE TO**

```
must：
├── 强制义务（内在，来自说话者）：
│   You must stop smoking. （你必须戒烟——我的要求）
├── 强烈推测（逻辑必然，~95%）：
│   She's been studying all night—she must be exhausted.
└── 否定 mustn't = 禁止：You mustn't enter this area.

have to：
├── 外在义务（规定/规则）：
│   I have to wear a uniform at work. （公司规定）
├── 可用各种时态：
│   I had to work late yesterday.
│   She will have to take the exam again.
└── 否定 don't have to = 不必要：
    You don't have to come if you're busy.
    （don't have to ≠ mustn't！前者是"不必"，后者是"禁止"）

must vs. have to 关键区别：
    I must study.     （我自己觉得应该学，内在驱动）
    I have to study.  （有考试/作业等外在要求）
```

**WILL / WOULD**

```
will：
├── 未来（预测/意愿）：She will arrive tomorrow.
├── 意愿（Volition）：I will help you.（我愿意帮你）
├── 习惯性行为（现在/将来）：Oil will float on water.（油总是浮在水面上）
└── 请求（非正式）：Will you open the door?

would：
├── 过去的习惯：When I was young, I would walk to school.
├── 委婉请求（比 will 更礼貌）：Would you mind closing the window?
├── 条件句主句（虚拟语气）：I would help if I could.
├── would like to = want to（礼貌）：I would like a cup of tea.
└── 过去将来时：She said she would come.
```

**SHALL / SHOULD**

```
shall（英式英语更常见）：
├── 第一人称将来（正式）：I shall return.
├── 提议/征求意见：Shall we go? Shall I open the door?
└── 正式规定（法律/合同）：The contractor shall complete by June.

should：
├── 建议/义务（~80% 的道义确定性）：You should see a doctor.
├── 期望（预期）：She should be here by now.
├── 条件句中替代 if（正式）：Should you need help, call me.
│   （= If you should need help...）
└── should + have + pp（对过去的遗憾）：
    You should have told me earlier. （你早该告诉我的）
```

**OUGHT TO vs. SHOULD：**

```
基本含义相近，但：
- should 更常用，语气更自然
- ought to 语气稍强，暗含"道义上应该"
    You ought to apologize.   （你应当道歉，道义上）
    You should apologize.     （你应该道歉，建议）
```

### 7.3.3 情态动词的时间坐标图

```
确定性强弱（对现在/将来的推测）：
    must > will > should > may > might/could

    He must be at home.     （我肯定他在家，95%）
    He will be at home.     （他应该在家，90%+）
    He should be at home.   （他应该在家，80%）
    He may be at home.      （他可能在家，50%）
    He might be at home.    （他或许在家，30%）
    He could be at home.    （他可能在家，30%，类似 might）

对过去的推测（情态动词 + have + 过去分词）：
    She must have left.     （她肯定已经离开了）
    She should have called. （她应该打电话来的，但她没有）
    She may have forgotten. （她可能忘了）
    She might have arrived. （她或许到了）
    She can't have done it. （她不可能做了这件事）
```

## 7.4 非谓语动词入门

非谓语动词（Non-finite Verbs）是不作谓语的动词形式，保留了动词的一些特性（可带宾语、状语等），但在句中充当名词、形容词或副词。

### 7.4.1 三种非谓语动词形式

```
不定式（Infinitive）：to + 动词原形
    to read, to write, to understand

动名词（Gerund）：动词 + -ing（名词功能）
    reading, writing, understanding

分词（Participle）：
├── 现在分词（Present Participle）：动词 + -ing（形容词/副词功能）
└── 过去分词（Past Participle）：动词 + -ed / 第三列变化形式（形容词/副词功能）
```

### 7.4.2 不定式 vs. 动名词的选择

这是中国学生的高频难点，以下是核心记忆规律：

```
只接动名词（v. + doing）的常见动词：
    enjoy, finish, avoid, consider, suggest, recommend,
    practice, admit, deny, postpone, quit, mind, resist,
    keep (on), give up, put off, carry on

    She enjoys reading.            I finished writing the report.
    He avoided making eye contact. They suggested taking a break.

只接不定式（v. + to do）的常见动词：
    want, wish, hope, decide, plan, promise, refuse,
    agree, offer, attempt, fail, manage, pretend, tend,
    expect, seem, appear, afford, dare

    She wants to leave.            He decided to stay.
    They refused to cooperate.     I managed to finish on time.

两者都可接，但含义不同（重要！）：
    remember + doing   = 记得曾经做过某事（已发生）
    remember + to do   = 记得要做某事（未发生）
    I remember locking the door. （我记得我锁了门——已锁）
    Remember to lock the door.   （记得锁门——提醒你去锁）

    forget + doing     = 忘记曾经做过
    forget + to do     = 忘记去做某事
    I forgot meeting him before.  （我忘记曾经见过他）
    I forgot to meet him.         （我忘记去见他了）

    stop + doing       = 停止做某事
    stop + to do       = 停下来去做另一件事
    He stopped smoking.           （他戒烟了）
    He stopped to smoke.          （他停下来抽了根烟）

    try + doing        = 试试看（尝试一种方法）
    try + to do        = 努力尝试（尽力但未必成功）
    Try adding some salt.         （试试加点盐）
    I tried to open the jar but couldn't. （我努力想开那个罐子但没成功）
```

## 7.5 短语动词（Phrasal Verbs）体系

短语动词（Phrasal Verbs）是英语中最具特色的语言现象之一，由动词与介词或副词（有时两者都有）组合而成，整体意义往往与组成部分的字面意思差异很大。

### 7.5.1 短语动词的四种结构类型

```
类型1：动词 + 副词（不及物）
    break down（抛锚/崩溃）：My car broke down.
    give up（放弃）：She gave up.
    turn up（出现）：He finally turned up.
    work out（结果/锻炼）：It all worked out fine.

类型2：动词 + 副词（及物，可分离）
    turn off（关掉）：Turn off the light. / Turn the light off.
    pick up（接人/捡起）：Pick up the kids. / Pick the kids up.
    put off（推迟）：Put off the meeting. / Put the meeting off.
    take off（脱掉）：Take off your shoes. / Take your shoes off.
    ⚠️ 注意：代词必须放中间！
    Turn it off. ✓    ❌ Turn off it.

类型3：动词 + 介词（及物，不可分离）
    look after（照顾）：She looks after her parents.
    deal with（处理）：How do you deal with stress?
    come across（偶然发现）：I came across this article.
    look into（调查）：They will look into the matter.

类型4：动词 + 副词 + 介词（三元短语动词）
    come up with（想出）：She came up with a great idea.
    put up with（忍受）：I can't put up with this noise.
    look forward to（期待）：I look forward to seeing you.
    run out of（用完）：We ran out of coffee.
    get along with（相处）：He gets along with everyone.
```

### 7.5.2 核心短语动词主题分类

```
关于 get 的短语动词（最多！）：
    get up（起床）        get along（相处）
    get over（恢复/克服）  get through（完成/通过）
    get rid of（摆脱）    get away with（逍遥法外）
    get back（回来/还回） get into（涉入/进入）
    get by（勉强过活）    get at（想说/够到）

关于 turn 的短语动词：
    turn on/off（开/关）   turn up（调高/出现）
    turn down（拒绝/调低） turn out（结果是/关掉）
    turn into（变成）      turn around（扭转局面）

关于 take 的短语动词：
    take off（脱/起飞/腾飞）   take on（承担/雇用）
    take up（开始从事/占用）    take in（欺骗/吸收）
    take over（接管）           take after（像某人）
    take back（收回）           take apart（拆开）
```

## 7.6 副词的分类与功能

副词（Adverb）可以修饰动词、形容词、其他副词，甚至整个句子，是语言中最"灵活"的词类之一。

### 7.6.1 副词的六大类型

**1. 时间副词（Adverbs of Time）**

```
具体时间：    yesterday, today, tomorrow, now, then, soon, recently,
             eventually, already, still, yet, just
频率时间（见7.6.5）：always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never

He has already finished.     （已经完成）
She hasn't called yet.       （还没打电话——用于否定/疑问）
I just saw him.              （刚刚看到他）
They will eventually succeed.（他们终将成功）
```

**2. 地点副词（Adverbs of Place）**

```
here, there, everywhere, nowhere, somewhere, anywhere,
inside, outside, upstairs, downstairs, nearby, abroad,
ahead, behind, above, below, away, home（不加 to）

Come here.        She went abroad.       He is upstairs.
The dog ran away. They live nearby.      I'm going home.
```

**3. 方式副词（Adverbs of Manner）**

```
大多数以 -ly 结尾（形容词 + ly）：
    quickly, slowly, carefully, beautifully, badly, softly,
    loudly, clearly, seriously, honestly, kindly

不规则方式副词：
    well（good 的副词形式）   He sings well.
    fast（形容词和副词同形）  She runs fast.
    hard（形容词和副词同形）  He works hard.
    early（形容词和副词同形）She arrived early.
    late（形容词和副词同形） The train arrived late.
```

**4. 程度副词（Adverbs of Degree）**

```
加强程度：
    very, extremely, absolutely, totally, completely, utterly,
    terribly, awfully, incredibly, enormously, remarkably

减弱程度：
    quite, fairly, rather, pretty, somewhat, relatively,
    slightly, a little, barely, hardly, scarcely, almost

特殊：too vs. enough
    too + 形容词/副词 = 过于（暗含负面）：It's too hot.
    形容词/副词 + enough = 足够...：It's warm enough.
    too...to...（太...以至于不能）：He is too young to vote.
    ...enough to...（足够...以至于能）：She is old enough to drive.
```

**5. 频率副词（Adverbs of Frequency）**

| 频率 | 副词 | 大约频率 |
|------|------|----------|
| 100% | always | 总是 |
| 90% | usually / normally | 通常 |
| 80% | often / frequently | 经常 |
| 60% | sometimes | 有时候 |
| 30% | occasionally | 偶尔 |
| 10% | seldom / rarely | 很少 |
| 5% | hardly ever / scarcely ever | 几乎从不 |
| 0% | never | 从不 |

**6. 评论/语气副词（Sentence Adverbs）**

```
这类副词修饰整个句子，表示说话者的态度：
    Fortunately, no one was hurt.     （幸运的是）
    Honestly, I don't know.           （说实话）
    Clearly, she is talented.         （显然）
    Apparently, he resigned.          （据说/显然）
    Surprisingly, they agreed.        （出乎意料的是）
    Obviously, something went wrong.  （显然）
```

## 7.7 副词的位置规则

副词的位置是英语学习者的难点，不同类型的副词有不同的位置偏好。

### 7.7.1 位置三大区域

```
句首（Front Position）：引起注意，常加逗号
    Yesterday, I met an old friend.
    Fortunately, everyone survived.
    In the morning, she practices yoga.

句中（Mid Position）：最常见位置
    位置细则：
    a) 在 be 动词之后：She is always punctual.
    b) 在第一个助动词之后：He has never been abroad.
    c) 在实义动词之前：She often reads before bed.
    ❌ She reads often before bed.（方式副词才能放句末，频率副词通常不这样）

句末（End Position）：最自然，是"未标记"位置
    She studies hard.
    He arrived early.
    They spoke quietly.
```

### 7.7.2 各类副词的位置偏好

```
频率副词 → 句中（be/助动词后，实义动词前）
    She always gets up at 6.
    He has never eaten sushi.
    They are rarely late.

方式副词 → 句末（最自然），也可句首
    She spoke softly.
    Carefully, he opened the package.

时间副词 → 句末或句首（不在句中）
    ✓  She called yesterday.
    ✓  Yesterday she called.
    ❌ She yesterday called.

程度副词 → 紧靠被修饰的词前面
    She is very happy.        （very 在 happy 前）
    He almost missed the bus. （almost 在 missed 前）
    They completely forgot.   （completely 在 forgot 前）

语气副词 → 句首（加逗号），或句末
    Fortunately, nobody was hurt.
    Nobody was hurt, fortunately.
```

### 7.7.3 副词位置影响语义的案例

```
位置不同，意义不同！

Only she called me.       （只有她给我打了电话——其他人没打）
She only called me.       （她只是给我打了电话——没做别的）
She called only me.       （她只给我打了电话——没给别人打）

I almost drove the wrong car.  （我差点开了别人的车）
I drove almost the wrong car.  （我开了一辆几乎是错误的车——奇怪）

Even she understood.      （就连她都理解了——她通常不理解）
She even understood.      （她甚至理解了——超出预期）
```

## 7.8 易混淆的形容词与副词对

这是中国学生的高频错误区，以下是最重要的对比：

### 7.8.1 同形词：形容词和副词同形

```
hard（形容词：硬的/困难的 vs. 副词：努力地/猛烈地）
    This is a hard question.     （形容词）
    She works hard.              （副词，努力地）
    It rained hard.              （副词，大力地）

hardly（副词：几乎不）—— 与 hard 意义完全不同！
    I hardly know him.           （我几乎不认识他）
    She hardly slept last night. （她昨晚几乎没睡）

late（形容词：晚的 vs. 副词：晚地）
    the late train                （形容词，晚班火车）
    He arrived late.              （副词，晚到了）

lately（副词：最近）—— 近义于 recently
    Have you seen her lately?     （你最近见过她吗？）
    I haven't been feeling well lately.

near（形容词：近的 vs. 介词/副词：在...附近）
    We live near the park.       （介词用法）
    The exam is near.            （形容词）

nearly（副词：几乎）—— 近义于 almost
    I nearly forgot.             （我差点忘了）
    She is nearly 30.            （她快30岁了）
```

### 7.8.2 完整对比表

| 形容词 | 副词（-ly） | 含义对比 |
|--------|------------|----------|
| hard | hard / hardly | hard=努力; hardly=几乎不 |
| late | late / lately | late=晚; lately=最近 |
| near | near / nearly | near=近; nearly=几乎 |
| high | high / highly | high=在高处; highly=高度地/非常 |
| deep | deep / deeply | deep=深地（物理）; deeply=深深地（情感） |
| close | close / closely | close=近地; closely=仔细地/密切地 |
| wide | wide / widely | wide=宽地（wide open）; widely=广泛地 |
| fair | fair / fairly | fair=公平地; fairly=相当地 |
| free | free / freely | free=免费; freely=自由地 |
| pretty | pretty / prettily | pretty（副词）=相当（口语）; prettily=漂亮地 |

**例句对比：**

```
high vs. highly：
    The bird flew high.          （鸟飞得很高——物理高度）
    She is highly respected.     （她备受尊重——程度副词）
    I think highly of her work.  （我对她的工作评价很高）

deep vs. deeply：
    The well is 20 meters deep.  （井深20米）
    Dig deep into the earth.     （向地底深处挖）
    She was deeply moved.        （她深受感动——情感上）
    I deeply regret my mistake.  （我深感遗憾）

close vs. closely：
    She sat close to me.         （她坐在我旁边——物理距离近）
    They watched closely.        （他们仔细观察——仔细地）
    He is closely related to her.（他与她关系密切）
```

## 练习

### 练习一：情态动词语义辨析

选择最合适的情态动词：

1. You ___ (must/should) wear a seatbelt—it's the law.
2. She ___ (can/could) speak three languages when she was ten.
3. I ___ (may/must) have left my keys at the office—I'm not sure.
4. You ___ (mustn't/don't have to) come if you're busy—it's optional.
5. The package ___ (should/must) arrive by tomorrow—I sent it three days ago.
6. ___ (Shall/Will) we go for a walk? （提议）
7. I ___ (would/used to) walk to school when I was young.

### 练习二：动名词 vs. 不定式

用动名词（-ing）或不定式（to do）填入括号中的动词：

1. She enjoys ___ (read) mystery novels.
2. He decided ___ (leave) early to avoid traffic.
3. I remember ___ (meet) him at the conference last year. （已经发生）
4. Don't forget ___ (lock) the door when you leave. （提醒未来的动作）
5. They suggested ___ (take) a different route.
6. She stopped ___ (smoke) five years ago.
7. He stopped ___ (have) a rest during the hike. （停下来做另一件事）

### 练习三：副词位置

改正下列句子中副词位置的错误（如有），或说明句子正确：

1. She speaks quietly always.
2. He never has been to Japan.
3. I almost missed the train yesterday.
4. They carefully examined the evidence.
5. He works hard always.

### 练习四：形容词 vs. 副词

选择正确的形式：

1. She works (hard / hardly) and rarely takes breaks.
2. He (hard / hardly) ate anything at dinner.
3. They live (near / nearly) the school—just a 5-minute walk.
4. We've (near / nearly) finished the project.
5. The artist is (high / highly) skilled in her craft.
6. The plane is flying (high / highly) above the clouds.

**参考答案：**

练习一：1. must  2. could  3. may  4. don't have to  5. should  6. Shall  7. would/used to

练习二：1. reading  2. to leave  3. meeting  4. to lock  5. taking  6. smoking  7. to have

练习三：
1. ❌ → She always speaks quietly.
2. ❌ → He has never been to Japan.
3. ✓（almost 在句中可以，修饰 missed）
4. ✓（carefully 在句首/句中均可）
5. ❌ → He always works hard.

练习四：1. hard  2. hardly  3. near  4. nearly  5. highly  6. high

## 本章小结

| 知识点 | 核心规律 | 记忆要点 |
|--------|----------|----------|
| 系动词 | 连接主语与表语，可用 be 替换测试 | look/sound/smell/taste/feel + 形容词（非副词） |
| 及物/不及物 | Vt 需宾语；Vi 不需要；注意 lie/lay 区别 | listen to, arrive at（不及物动词需加介词） |
| 情态动词 | 9 个主要情态动词各有语义层次 | must（内在）vs. have to（外在）；mustn't vs. don't have to |
| 动名词/不定式 | 关键动词后接哪种形式须记忆；remember/forget/stop/try 后两种均可但意义不同 | try doing vs. try to do |
| 短语动词 | 可分离（代词需放中间）vs. 不可分离 | come up with, put up with, run out of（不可分离）|
| 副词分类 | 6 类：时间/地点/方式/程度/频率/语气 | only/even 位置影响语义 |
| 副词位置 | 频率副词→句中；方式副词→句末；时间副词→句末/句首 | 不能放在 be 动词前 |
| 形容词/副词对 | hard/hardly, late/lately, near/nearly, high/highly | 加 -ly 后意义可能完全不同 |

> **学习建议：** 动词是英语学习的心脏。建议专门建立一本"动词笔记本"，记录：（1）动词搭配的及物/不及物用法；（2）接动名词还是不定式；（3）重要的短语动词及例句。坚持三个月，动词掌握程度将大幅提升。
